The Chinese language

The main language spoken in China is Mandarin. The writing is in simplified Chinese.
It used to be purely traditional Chinese but the Chinese government changed the writing to
simplified Chinese during 1955 because too many Chinese people were illiterate so they decided
to simplify the language. Most Chinese characters were simplified but some of them stayed as their traditional form
because they could not be simplified any further.
Generally, some people know traditional Chinese but nearly everyone knows simplified Chinese in Mainland China.


When it comes to Hong Kong and Taiwan, they mainly teach and use traditional Chinese. Hong Kong people speak Cantonese
and people from Taiwan speak Taiwanese. They cannot understand each other vocally unless if they already know each other's
language. This goes for Mandarin-speaking people as well. People from Hong Kong will still see or may even use simplified Chinese
due to China's influence on it. Due to politics, there may be further action to push simplified Chinese onto Hong Kong, but they
traditionally use traditional Chinese.


When writing simplified Chinese or traditional Chinese, it is best practice to follow a stroke order to replicate the charatcer
completely. Due to the digtial revolution of the world, it is becoming less important to follow stroke orders because
you can just type the character out completely without actually writing it. However, stroke order is an important part
of the culture and it is the foundation of calligraphy which is an artform of our characters.
